Multi-Site Security Governance

Multi-country logistics network

Building a Multi-Country Security Governance Framework

Context: A logistics operation spanning several countries managed security locally, site by site, with no shared view of risk across the network.

Challenge: Audit expectations, third-party controls and risk reporting differed by region, making oversight and comparison difficult.

  • Designed a single security governance framework across all regional operations.
  • Harmonised risk visibility, audit expectations and third-party controls.
  • Established shared reporting lines into management.

Result: More consistent compliance across regions, faster audit follow-up and clearer accountability for supplier controls.

Audit & Assurance

Internal operations & 30+ providers

Designing a Security Audit Programme from Methodology to Remediation

Context: A network of internal operations and 30+ third-party providers needed dependable assurance, but audits were inconsistent and findings lingered.

Challenge: There was no shared methodology, evidence standard or accountable path from finding to verified closure.

  • Built an end-to-end audit methodology with clear evidence requirements.
  • Translated findings into owned remediation plans with verified closure.
  • Added escalation and supplier-performance routines.

Result: €1.2M savings, finding closure up from 25% to 55%, and 30% shorter audit cycles.

Data-Driven Risk Reporting

Loss prevention & security operations

Turning Security Data into Management Decisions

Context: Security and loss-prevention data sat in scattered logs and spreadsheets, too fragmented to guide decisions.

Challenge: Incidents, audit evidence, supplier performance and remediation were never connected into a single view.

  • Standardised the data into decision-grade dashboards.
  • Linked incidents, audit evidence, supplier performance and remediation.
  • Turned reporting into actionable management routines.

Result: 15% fewer incidents, 20% less shrinkage and around €2.3M in annual losses prevented.

Critical supplier contract

Detecting a Supplier Conflict of Interest in a Critical Contract

Context: A critical contract showed procurement irregularities that did not add up, raising questions about supplier integrity.

Challenge: The signals were scattered across procurement records, ownership data and subcontracting arrangements, with no clear picture.

  • Ran an evidence-led review connecting procurement deviations, ownership checks and subcontracting risk.
  • Documented the findings into a clear, defensible escalation package.
  • Defined the decision points for management and legal.

Result: A hidden conflict of interest surfaced and escalated with the evidence needed to act.

Workforce security operations

Reducing Security Friction Through Data and Stakeholder Engagement
