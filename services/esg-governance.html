The Distinction That Matters

ESG governance and ESG reporting are not the same thing.

ESG Reporting

Producing a sustainability report, a CSRD disclosure or an ESG questionnaire response. It describes what you have done and what you intend. It is backward-looking. Its credibility depends entirely on the quality of the governance beneath it.

Without governance, reporting is storytelling. With assurance requirements now embedded in CSRD, storytelling is a legal exposure.

ESG Governance

The system that decides who is accountable for each ESG topic, how data is collected and verified, how ESG risks are identified and managed, and how sustainability commitments are connected to operational decisions.

This is what makes the report defensible. This is what an assurance provider audits. This is what we build.

Who It's For

Organisations that want ESG to mean something internally.

Companies preparing for CSRD assurance

Limited assurance on CSRD reporting is not a documentation exercise — it requires evidence that your ESG data has governance behind it. Named owners, defined collection processes, review and sign-off trails. If your governance is weak, your assurance opinion will say so.

Organisations embedding ESG into strategy

Your leadership team has made ESG commitments — to investors, to regulators, to employees. Now they need a management system that tracks progress, surfaces risks and connects those commitments to what actually happens in operations. Governance is that system.

Companies responding to investor or lender ESG requirements

PE-backed companies, companies seeking green financing or sustainability-linked loans, or organisations subject to investor ESG due diligence need to demonstrate governance substance — not just a policy document and a reported KPI.

What We Build

An ESG governance model your organisation can operate.

Every element is designed to be maintained by your internal team — not to require quarterly external support to keep running.

Accountability

Who is responsible for each ESG topic — at board level, at management level and operationally. A governance map that makes ESG ownership explicit rather than assumed, and connects it to existing roles rather than creating new ones.

Data

Where each ESG data point comes from, who collects it, how it is verified and how it flows into reporting. Data ownership, collection procedures, quality checks and a clear audit trail from source to disclosure.

Controls

Internal controls over sustainability information — the review, sign-off and challenge mechanisms that give assurance providers confidence that your reported figures are reliable and your commitments are being monitored.

An ESG governance map: topics, owners, accountability at each organisational level.

A data ownership register: collection source, responsible owner, verification method, cadence.

Internal controls over sustainability reporting — designed to satisfy limited assurance requirements.

An ESG management cadence: how topics are reviewed, how risks are escalated, how progress is tracked.

Policy-to-process alignment: sustainability policies connected to the operational procedures that implement them.

A governance narrative for your assurance provider, investor or lender that explains the system, not just the numbers.

ESG & Enterprise Risk

ESG risks belong in your risk register — not in a separate sustainability framework.

ESG Governance · Risk integration

The organisations that manage ESG most effectively are those that treat it as a risk and control question, not a communications exercise. Climate transition risk, social licence risk, supply-chain human rights exposure, governance failures — these are business risks that belong in your enterprise risk framework alongside operational, financial and compliance risks.

We integrate ESG risk identification into your existing risk governance model. That means ESG topics assessed using the same methodology, owned by named individuals, reviewed on the same cadence and escalated through the same channels. One risk framework. One risk language. No parallel universe.

This integration is also what CSRD and lenders are looking for. When your ESG governance is embedded in your enterprise risk management, it demonstrates maturity — not box-ticking.

If you already have a risk framework, we extend it to cover ESG. If you are building both at the same time, we design them together.
