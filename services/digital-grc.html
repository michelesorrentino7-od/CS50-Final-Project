The Tool Myth

Your Excel is not the problem. Your process is.

Organisations invest in GRC platforms and six months later are maintaining the same data in the platform and in parallel spreadsheets, because the platform does not reflect how they actually work. The tool was designed around an ideal process that the organisation was never going to adopt.

The right sequence is: design the governance process first, then choose the technology that supports it. Not the other way around. We work with your actual environment — Microsoft 365, SharePoint, Notion, Google Workspace, or sector-specific tools — and build GRC workflows that people will use because they make the job easier, not harder.

Signs your digital GRC needs work

  • Risk registers that are updated once a year for the audit
  • Control evidence scattered across email threads and shared drives
  • Action tracking done in meeting notes, not a system
  • Management reporting built manually every quarter from multiple sources
  • An expensive platform that four people use and twenty ignore

What We Do

Lean digital workflows for risk, compliance and evidence.

Map

Understand your current data flows: where GRC information lives, who updates it, how it gets to management and where the gaps and duplications are. Most organisations are surprised by what they already have.

Design

Define the workflow: control register structure, evidence library logic, action tracking model, reporting cadence and dashboard design. Built around your tools, your team and your actual governance cadence.

Automate

Where repetitive tasks can be automated — evidence collection, monitoring alerts, status updates, report generation — we apply the right level of automation: enough to reduce manual work, not so much that the process breaks when someone changes a cell.

AI & Data Analysis

Applied carefully. Not demonstrated.

Digital GRC · AI-assisted monitoring and analysis

AI has genuine applications in GRC — anomaly detection in transaction data, automated classification of compliance evidence, pattern recognition in incident logs, contract review for risk language, and monitoring of regulatory change feeds. We use these tools where they reduce effort and improve accuracy.

What we do not do: recommend AI solutions to clients who have not yet solved the underlying data and process problems. AI applied to disorganised data produces disorganised outputs faster. The governance process has to work first.

We bring hands-on experience with data analysis, GRC platform configuration and AI-assisted workflows. We apply them in proportion to the problem — and we explain clearly what each tool does and does not do before recommending it.

Our standard: if the tool cannot be maintained by your team without our involvement six months after the engagement ends, it is the wrong tool.
