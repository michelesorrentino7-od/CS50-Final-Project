What This Is

A trusted governance presence. Not a subscription to advice.

The fractional model is well-established in finance (fractional CFO) and technology (fractional CTO). It is now increasingly recognised in governance, risk and compliance — because the need for senior GRC judgement does not wait for a company to reach the size where it can justify a full-time GRC director.

This is not a retained advisory service where you receive a newsletter and occasional calls. It is a structured monthly engagement where we are part of your governance rhythm — attending relevant meetings, reviewing what is happening, prioritising what matters and keeping the right things moving.

We carry the context of your business from month to month. That continuity is what makes the role valuable.

Who It's For

Organisations at the governance inflection point.

Companies between €5M and €50M in revenue

You are past startup. Investors, banks, enterprise clients and regulators are starting to ask governance questions. You do not yet have — or need — a full GRC function. But you need someone who can answer those questions credibly and keep things on track.

Foreign companies with Italian operations

Your head office handles governance globally. But Italy has specific requirements — Mod. 231, Garante, sector-specific local obligations — that need someone on the ground who understands both the local environment and how to connect it to your global framework.

Organisations building toward a transaction or financing

A governance gap identified in due diligence is expensive. A Fractional GRC Officer engaged twelve months before a process gives you time to build substance — not to scramble to create documentation that did not exist three weeks ago.

How It Works

A governance cadence, not a project timeline.

Every engagement is shaped around the organisation's actual governance rhythm. There is no standard package — we agree the scope, cadence and focus areas together at the start, and review them as the business evolves.

Monthly

A governance review meeting with leadership. Risk and control priorities, open actions, upcoming decisions with governance implications, and any regulatory changes that require a response. Clear, documented, consistent.

Ongoing

Available as a sounding board between meetings. When something happens — a regulator enquiry, a supplier incident, a board question, a contract with unusual governance implications — you have someone to call who knows your business.

Quarterly

A broader review of governance posture: what has moved, what has not, what is coming and what needs to be resourced. A written summary suitable for board or investor review.

We attend meetings as a participant, not a presenter of slides. We carry institutional memory across months and years, not across an engagement team. We escalate what matters. We filter what does not.

An Important Distinction

This is not a project with a final report.

Fractional GRC Officer · What makes it different

Consulting projects have deliverables, timelines and endings. A Fractional GRC Officer has a relationship. The value is not in the document produced at the end of the engagement — it is in the accumulated context, the decisions shaped along the way and the governance habits built into how the organisation actually runs.

KPMG will produce a gap analysis and a roadmap. We will be there when the roadmap meets operational reality and needs to change. We will sit with your CFO when the auditor asks an unexpected question. We will tell you, without a change request, when something we recommended three months ago is not working.

The organisations that get the most value from a Fractional GRC engagement are those that want a governance counterpart, not a compliance supplier. If that is you, we should talk.
