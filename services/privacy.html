The Reality

Privacy obligations fail when they stay legal abstractions.

Most organisations had a lawyer draft their privacy policy and a web agency add a consent banner. That covers perhaps 10% of what GDPR actually requires. The remaining 90% is operational: which data your HR, sales and operations teams handle every day, which vendors receive it, who is responsible when something goes wrong, and how you would demonstrate all of this to a regulator tomorrow morning.

The Italian Garante is one of the most active data protection authorities in Europe. Enforcement is real, and the investigations we have seen most frequently start not with a data breach but with an employee complaint or a routine audit that finds no evidence of accountability.

We work from the inside out — from the data to the documentation, not the other way around.

Operational Privacy

Privacy that lives in how your team works, not in a folder.

Map

Understand what personal data you process, where it comes from, who has access, how long you keep it and which vendors receive it. A data map is the foundation of every other privacy decision.

Assign

Give each processing activity a named owner. GDPR accountability means someone in the organisation must be able to answer: why do we have this data, and what would we do if a regulator asked about it?

Embed

Turn the framework into operational routines — incident escalation, data subject request handling, vendor review, retention enforcement — so privacy becomes part of how decisions are made, not a separate compliance exercise.

DPO Support

Data Protection Officer — designated or supported.

Privacy & Data Protection · DPO function

Not every organisation is required to appoint a DPO — but many should, and most that do appoint one underinvest in giving the function real substance. A DPO without access to operational data processing information, without a channel to management and without time to do proactive work is a liability, not an asset.

We support DPOs who are already in post but need practical governance infrastructure — data maps, RoPA, DPIA templates, incident logs, vendor review checklists. We also support organisations that need to designate a DPO and want that function set up properly from the start.

We do not act as your DPO — the independence requirement matters. We build the programme that makes your DPO effective.
