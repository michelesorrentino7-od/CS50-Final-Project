## 2. Expanded case studies (problem → approach → outcome)

Each card keeps its title and uses the `Context / Challenge / steps / Result` shape
already on the page. Service links target the real `services/*.html` detail pages.

### A — ERM Framework Expansion · `risk-governance` *(existing, kept)*
A fast-moving logistics network needed better enterprise risk visibility, but operational,
QHSSE and strategic risks were tracked separately with no single view. We expanded the risk
taxonomy and ownership model, introduced KRI routines and control expectations, and aligned
remediation tracking with management reporting. **Result: 40% broader risk coverage and 25%
compliance-gap closure within 18 months.**
**Services:** Governance & Risk (risk taxonomy) · Digital GRC (KRI reporting) · Assurance & Security (control governance).

### C — Building a Multi-Country Security Governance Framework · `multi-site`
A logistics operation spanning several countries managed security locally, site by site,
with no shared view of risk across the network — audit expectations, third-party controls and
risk reporting differed by region. We designed a single security-governance framework across
all regional operations, harmonised risk visibility, audit expectations and third-party
controls, and established shared reporting lines into management. **Result: more consistent
compliance across regions, faster audit follow-up and clearer accountability for supplier controls.**
**Services:** Governance & Risk (risk visibility) · Assurance & Security (third-party oversight) · Fractional GRC (advisory model).

### D — Designing a Security Audit Programme from Methodology to Remediation · `audit-assurance` *(folds Third-Party Audit metrics)*
A network of internal operations and 30+ third-party providers needed dependable assurance,
but audits were inconsistent and findings lingered, with no shared methodology, evidence
standard or accountable path to verified closure. We built an end-to-end audit methodology
with clear evidence requirements, translated findings into owned remediation plans with
verified closure, and added escalation and supplier-performance routines. **Result: €1.2M
savings, finding closure up from 25% to 55%, and 30% shorter audit cycles.**
**Services:** Assurance & Security (audit programme) · Governance & Risk (remediation governance) · Compliance & Regulation (evidence).

### E — Detecting a Supplier Conflict of Interest in a Critical Contract · `third-party`
A critical contract showed procurement irregularities that did not add up, raising questions
about supplier integrity — but the signals were scattered across procurement records, ownership
data and subcontracting arrangements. We ran an evidence-led review connecting procurement
deviations, ownership checks and subcontracting risk, documented the findings into a clear,
defensible escalation package, and defined the decision points for management and legal.
**Result: a hidden conflict of interest surfaced and escalated with the evidence needed to act.**
**Services:** Assurance & Security (investigation) · Compliance & Regulation (procurement integrity) · Governance & Risk (escalation).

### F — Standardising Incident Management Across a Logistics Network · `incident-crisis`
Across a logistics network, sites handled incidents their own way, so escalation, evidence and
investigation quality varied widely and serious events were governed inconsistently. We designed
a network incident-management guideline covering escalation and evidence, standardised
investigation workflows and closure governance, and aligned the model to operational reporting.
**Result: consistent incident handling across the network and a clearer, more accountable path to closure.**
**Services:** Assurance & Security (incident ops) · Governance & Risk (operating model) · Compliance & Regulation (policy design).

### G — Building Dawn Raid Readiness Across 35 Sites · `incident-crisis`
A group operating 35 sites across 7 countries had no consistent way to respond to a dawn raid or
comparable legal inspection, and unclear procedures risked slow notification and wrong escalations.
We implemented dawn-raid readiness procedures across all 35 sites, defined notification and
escalation paths, and trained local teams to respond with confidence. **Result: faster
notification and fewer wrong escalations, with consistent readiness across 7 countries.**
**Services:** Compliance & Regulation (legal response) · Assurance & Security (readiness & training) · Fractional GRC (advisory).

### H — Standardising Security Requirements for 3PL Launches · `third-party`
New third-party logistics (3PL) sites went live with security requirements interpreted
differently each time, and control expectations were not measurable before go-live, so
non-conformities surfaced after launch. We created a 3PL security implementation protocol,
made supplier control expectations measurable before go-live, and built launch-readiness checks
into onboarding. **Result: stronger launch readiness and fewer non-conformities at new 3PL sites.**
**Services:** CSRD / Supply Chain (supplier control) · Assurance & Security (go-live readiness) · Governance & Risk (implementation governance).

### I — Turning Security Data into Management Decisions · `data-reporting` *(folds Loss Prevention metrics)*
Security and loss-prevention data sat in scattered logs and spreadsheets, too fragmented to guide
decisions — incidents, audit evidence, supplier performance and remediation were never connected.
We standardised the data into decision-grade dashboards, linked incidents, audit evidence,
supplier performance and remediation, and turned reporting into actionable management routines.
**Result: 15% fewer incidents, 20% less shrinkage and around €2.3M in annual losses prevented.**
**Services:** Digital GRC (dashboards) · Assurance & Security (loss prevention) · Governance & Risk (KPI/KRI).

### J — Reducing Security Friction Through Data and Stakeholder Engagement · `behavioural`
Security rules were creating friction with employees, lowering acceptance and fuelling workplace
disputes, because controls were seen as obstacles rather than shared practice. We combined
incident analytics with employee feedback and workshops, used live dashboards to make the
reasoning visible, and reworked rules around how people actually work. **Result: higher rule
acceptance and fewer workplace disputes, with security seen as shared practice.**
**Services:** Assurance & Security (security culture) · Digital GRC (analytics) · Fractional GRC (stakeholder engagement).

### B — GDPR Readiness · `privacy-compliance` *(existing, kept)*
Operations needed readiness across data, sustainability and compliance evidence, but evidence
owners and operational controls were not consistently mapped. We mapped readiness gaps against
operational processes, defined evidence expectations for internal owners, and connected privacy
and supplier-assurance routines. **Result: a stronger readiness view, clearer evidence ownership
and reusable compliance routines.**
**Services:** Privacy (data protection) · Compliance & Regulation (evidence) · CSRD / Supply Chain (supplier assurance).

## 3. Image research — candidates for the new slots

**Direction (non-negotiable):** warm stone / aged-paper palette, architectural, calm, editorial.
Avoid corporate stock, loud color, tech clichés, watermarks. All framed images on the site are
auto-muted (`saturate(0.72) contrast(0.94)`) and cropped to a fixed strip, so composition that
survives a centre crop matters more than exact ratio. **Target 4:3, ~1400px long side, JPG q≈82,
< 400KB** (per the `images.js` recipe).

**License check before use:** Unsplash License and Pexels License both allow commercial use with
no attribution. Skip any "Unsplash+" premium result, and on Wikimedia confirm the specific
file's licence. Verify each photo on its own page before downloading.

> have photos and need nothing. The **6 slots below** are empty — their frames hide gracefully
> until a JPG with the exact filename lands in `images/middle/`.

## 4. How to wire an image in (per `images.js` recipe)
1. Download a chosen photo (check its licence on the page first).
2. Resize to ~1400px long side, export JPG quality ~82, keep it < 400KB.
3. Save it with the **exact filename** into `images/middle/`:
   `cases-multi-country.jpg` · `cases-supplier-coi.jpg` · `cases-incident.jpg` ·
   `cases-dawn-raid.jpg` · `cases-3pl.jpg` · `cases-friction.jpg`.
4. Hard-refresh — the registry slot is already wired, so the card lights up. No HTML/CSS edits.
   (If you want different alt text, edit the `alt` on the matching `<img data-img="…">` in
   `case-studies.html`.)
